Insurance Services in India

The Department of Financial Services oversees the insurance sector in India, promoting financial security and risk management through various insurance schemes. Our mission is to ensure accessible, affordable, and reliable insurance coverage for all citizens, supporting the vision of a financially inclusive India.

Explore the key insurance initiatives below, designed to cater to diverse needs, from health and life insurance to crop and property protection.

Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY)

A life insurance scheme offering coverage of ₹2 lakh at an affordable premium of ₹436 per annum. Available to individuals aged 18 to 50 years with a bank or post office account.

Learn More

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY)

An accident insurance scheme providing ₹2 lakh coverage for accidental death or permanent disability at a premium of ₹20 per annum. Open to individuals aged 18 to 70 years.

Learn More

Ayushman Bharat - PMJAY

The world’s largest health insurance scheme, offering up to ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care hospitalization to over 50 crore beneficiaries.

Learn More

Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)

A crop insurance scheme to protect farmers against crop losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases, with premiums as low as 1.5-2% for most crops.

Learn More

Atal Pension Yojana (APY)

A pension scheme ensuring a fixed pension of ₹1,000 to ₹5,000 per month for subscribers, aimed at providing financial security in old age, particularly for the unorganized sector.

Learn More

Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI)

The IRDAI regulates and promotes the growth of the insurance sector, ensuring its financial stability and protecting policyholders’ interests.

Learn More

Why Insurance Matters

Insurance is a critical tool for financial protection, helping individuals and families manage risks related to health, life, property, and agriculture. The Department of Financial Services is committed to expanding insurance penetration across India, ensuring that every citizen has access to affordable and comprehensive coverage.

  • Protects against unforeseen financial losses.
  • Promotes financial stability and peace of mind.
  • Supports economic growth by mitigating risks.
  • Encourages savings and long-term financial planning.
