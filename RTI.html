Right to Information (RTI)

About RTI Act 2005

The Right to Information Act, 2005 empowers citizens to seek information from public authorities, promoting transparency and accountability in governance. The Department of Financial Services is committed to providing information to citizens in accordance with the provisions of the RTI Act.

RTI applications can be filed online through the RTI Online Portal or submitted physically to the designated CPIO.

How to Apply for RTI

Online Method

  1. Visit the RTI Online Portal at rtionline.gov.in
  2. Click on "Submit Request" and register/login
  3. Fill the online application form
  4. Pay the fee of ₹10 through online payment gateway
  5. Submit the application and note the registration number

Offline Method

  1. Write an application on plain paper or use the prescribed format
  2. Address it to the Central Public Information Officer (CPIO)
  3. Attach a Postal Order/DD of ₹10 in favor of Accounts Officer, DFS
  4. Send by post or submit in person at the designated office

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can file RTI?

Any citizen of India can file an RTI application.

What is the time limit for response?

30 days from receipt of application (35 days if transferred).

Can I file RTI in Hindi?

Yes, RTI applications can be filed in Hindi or English.
